WhatsApp is where your customers are. In Latin America, it's the dominant communication channel — more than email, more than website forms, more than any other platform.

The problem is that handling WhatsApp at scale is expensive. Someone has to be available, read every message, evaluate what the client needs, and respond. All the time.

A well-designed AI agent can change that. But it's important to understand what it can do and what it can't.

## What it does well

**Answering frequent questions**
Hours, prices, available services, how the process works, what documents they need. All of this can live in the agent and respond in seconds, 24 hours a day.

**Capturing lead information**
Name, contact, what they need. The agent can collect this information naturally and organize it for the sales team.

**Qualifying inquiries**
Not every message deserves the same attention. The agent can ask qualifying questions and determine what type of case it is before passing it to a human.

**Escalating when needed**
A good agent knows when it can't help. In those cases, it transfers the conversation to the human team with the relevant context already captured.

## What it doesn't do well

**Complex negotiations**
If the client has a special case requiring deep judgment and context, the agent isn't the place for that.

**Emotional relationships**
When a client is upset or frustrated, the agent can contain the situation, but real resolution requires a person.

**Processes without clear rules**
An agent works well when there are defined rules. If every case is completely different and requires unique judgment, the agent has little value.

## Design matters more than technology

The most common mistake when implementing a WhatsApp agent is focusing too much on the technology and too little on conversation design.

Before building anything, you need to define:

- What questions it answers and which it escalates
- What tone and personality it has
- When it transfers to a human and how
- What it does outside business hours

A poorly designed agent frustrates customers. A well-designed one delights them.
